WHAT WOULD YOU LIKE TO DO?

Nainital is a pleasant hill station situated at an altitude of 1938 m in the Indian state of Uttarakhand and is the administrative headquarters of Nainital district in the Kumaon foothills of the outer Himalayas. Nainital appears in ancient legends and is the former summer capital of United Province. It is surrounded by a number of satellite tourist spots and subcentres.

  • Naini Peak (2611 m): It is a 6-km (2.5-hr) walk to the highest point in Nainital, which commands superb views of Nanda Devi and the Indian Himalayas.
  • Snow View (2270 m): At the end of this 2.5-km (1-hr) walk, you can enjoy a fine view of the Himalayan range through a powerful binocular telescope.
  • Kahala Garden (2265 m): The valley and slopes around Kahala Garden offer attractions to naturalists, painters, trekkers, and bird watchers. The area is rich in flora and fauna.
  • Camel's Back (2600 m): A 5.5-km walk through mature forest with pleasant views of the plains.
  • Kilbury (2215 m): A 12-km trek leads to a picnic spot at a government forest rest house with splendid sunset and Himalaya views. Abundant flora and fauna.
  • Khurpa Tal: 12 kilometers to a small picturesque lake for fishing and swimming, which is also a well-known picnic spot.
  • Kanchi Temple: 14 kilometers. This group of temples near a river was built by Baba Neam Korali, a famous philosopher. An enjoyable trek from Nainital through deep forest.
  • Hanuman Garhi (1950 m): 3.25 km to beautiful sunset views from this group of temples. Nearby is the state observatory, which can be visited by appointment on certain days.

TREKKING TO LOCAL VILLAGES

Experience Kumaoni village life and taste local dishes, see breath-taking sunrise and sunset views of the Himalayas, and enjoy the peace and quiet of the forests and the wonder of our wildlife.

Ghugukhan Trek (Rs 1500 per person, including basic Indian-style accommodation, all meals and teas):

Nainital to Tanki to Naini Peak to Pangot to Ghugukhan (overnight stay). The next morning, Ghughkhan to Pangot to Kilbury to Tanki and return to Nainital, a 28-km round trip.

Attractions include Ghanial Devi Temple hill, sunset from Harayal, an overnight stay with a local village family, home-cooked Kumaoni meals, magnificent views of the Himalayas and plains, wildlife and bird watching.

This is just one of the many possible treks around the Nainital area of Kumaon. Let us know what you would like to do and how long you would like to trek, and we can create a trekking experience just for you.
